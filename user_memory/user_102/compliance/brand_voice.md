# Brand Voice Guide

## Core Identity
Ampliflow content sounds like a sharp, thoughtful operator who has actually run a business. Not a marketer performing enthusiasm. Not a consultant hiding behind jargon. A real person who understands the grind and speaks plainly about it.

## Primary Tone Attributes

### Clear
Say the thing directly. No throat clearing, no preamble, no winding up to the point. The first sentence should earn attention. If a word does not add meaning cut it.

### Confident
Write from a position of genuine expertise. No hedging phrases like "it might be worth considering" or "you could perhaps try". State things. Own the perspective.

### Human
Real businesses are run by real people with real pressures. Content should acknowledge that. Warmth is not weakness. A sentence that sounds like it was written by a person beats a polished paragraph that sounds like a press release every time.

### Grounded
Every claim should feel earnable. No hyperbole. No empty superlatives. If something is good explain why it is good. Specificity beats enthusiasm.

## Tone by Client Type
Different ICP profiles require tone calibration within these core attributes:

- Local service businesses: warmer, more personal, community rooted
- Industrial and manufacturing: precise, formal, specification led
- Health and wellness: calm, reassuring, never alarmist
- Nonprofit and charity: empathetic, dignified, never exploitative
- Tech and AI founders: direct, intellectually honest, no buzzword stacking
- Education providers: authoritative but approachable, outcome focused
- E-commerce brands: aspirational, identity driven, energetic but not frantic
- Community organisations: inclusive, celebratory, member first

## Sentence and Structural Preferences
- Short sentences as the default, vary length deliberately for rhythm
- Active voice always, passive voice never unless intentional
- Paragraphs of three sentences maximum in social content
- Lead with the most important thing, context comes after
- Questions are powerful openers used sparingly
- Lists only when genuinely list shaped information, not as a lazy structure

## Banned Phrases and Patterns
- Game changer
- Cutting edge
- In today's fast paced world
- Unlock your potential
- Leverage
- Synergy
- Take your business to the next level
- We are excited to announce
- Delighted to share
- At the end of the day
- It goes without saying
- Needless to say
- In conclusion
- Touch base
- Move the needle
- Low hanging fruit
- Boilerplate calls to action with no specificity

## Punctuation and Formatting
- No exclamation marks unless the moment genuinely earns one, maximum one per piece
- Em dashes for emphasis and rhythm, used sparingly
- No ALL CAPS for emphasis, ever
- Hashtags only when platform appropriate, never stuffed
- Emojis only for platforms where they fit the ICP, never in professional B2B content
