# Historical Content Performance

## Overview
This file tracks the highest performing content pieces produced through Ampliflow. Each entry includes the content type, target ICP, platform, key metrics, and structural notes on why it worked. This data is used to inform future content generation by identifying patterns in what resonates with each audience.

## Top Performing Posts

### Post 001
**Client Type:** Local Service Business
**ICP Target:** Local Service Business Owner
**Platform:** Instagram
**Content Type:** Behind the scenes story
**Performance:** 847 likes, 134 comments, 62 shares, 4.2x average reach
**Published:** 2024-11-14

**Content Summary:**
A short narrative post following the owner of a halal catering business through a full day of preparation for a 200-person wedding event. Opened with a 4am alarm. Closed with the first plate served.

**Why It Worked:**
- Opened with a specific time and sensory detail, not a generic greeting
- Made the reader feel the scale of the work without listing tasks
- No call to action, the story sold itself
- Comments were almost entirely emotional responses and personal connections
- Shared heavily by people tagging friends who run similar businesses

**Reusable Pattern:**
Open with a specific moment in time. Follow one person through one high stakes day. End at the moment of delivery or completion. No explicit selling.

### Post 002
**Client Type:** Tech and AI Product
**ICP Target:** Tech and AI Founder
**Platform:** LinkedIn
**Content Type:** Contrarian opinion post
**Performance:** 12,400 impressions, 287 reactions, 94 comments, 31 reposts
**Published:** 2024-10-03

**Content Summary:**
A post arguing that most AI startups are solving distribution problems not technology problems, and that the founders who win will be the ones who understand their customer better than their model architecture.

**Why It Worked:**
- Led with a direct and slightly provocative claim
- Did not hedge or qualify the opening statement
- Generated disagreement in comments which drove algorithmic reach
- Attracted both supporters and challengers creating a real debate
- Founder shared it and added their own perspective in comments driving further engagement

**Reusable Pattern:**
State a contrarian but defensible position in the first line. Do not soften it. Let the body of the post build the case. End with an open question that invites response.

### Post 003
**Client Type:** Nonprofit and Charity
**ICP Target:** Charity Communications Lead
**Platform:** Facebook
**Content Type:** Impact milestone post
**Performance:** 2,100 reach, 341 reactions, 89 shares, 14 new donor signups directly attributed
**Published:** 2024-12-01

**Content Summary:**
A post marking the charity reaching 500 families supported in a single year. Led with the number. Followed with one anonymised family story told in three sentences. Closed with a direct donation link and a specific ask.

**Why It Worked:**
- The number was specific and credible not rounded or vague
- The human story gave the number emotional weight
- The ask was specific, fund one more family this December
- Dignity was maintained throughout, no imagery or language that reduced the family to their circumstances
- Shared heavily by existing volunteers and donors into their own networks

**Reusable Pattern:**
Lead with a specific verified number. Immediately follow with one human story that gives it weight. Make the call to action specific and time bounded. Maintain dignity throughout.

### Post 004
**Client Type:** E-Commerce and Retail
**ICP Target:** E-Commerce Brand Owner
**Platform:** Instagram
**Content Type:** Product launch teaser
**Performance:** 1,340 likes, 203 saves, 78 comments, 34% increase in link clicks week of launch
**Published:** 2025-01-20

**Content Summary:**
A three post teaser series for a new streetwear collection drop. Post one showed a single detail shot with no context. Post two showed the making process with one line of copy. Post three was the full reveal with pricing and link.

**Why It Worked:**
- Withholding information created genuine curiosity
- Each post rewarded the people who had been following the series
- The making process post humanised the product before the sell
- Comments on post one were almost entirely questions which were answered in post three
- Saves were high indicating people wanted to find the post again at launch

**Reusable Pattern:**
For product launches, withhold before you reveal. Three posts minimum. Detail, process, reveal. Each post should make the next one feel inevitable.

### Post 005
**Client Type:** Education and Training
**ICP Target:** Education and Training Provider
**Platform:** LinkedIn
**Content Type:** Student outcome story
**Performance:** 8,900 impressions, 412 reactions, 156 comments, 23 direct course enquiries
**Published:** 2025-02-11

**Content Summary:**
A post following a student who joined a data analytics bootcamp while working a full time job. Specific details about their schedule, the moment they nearly quit, and where they are six months later. Written in close third person with the student quoted directly.

**Why It Worked:**
- Specific details made the story feel real and verifiable
- The nearly quit moment created tension and made the outcome feel earned
- Direct quotes gave the student a voice rather than speaking for them
- The outcome was specific, job title, company type, salary band range, not vague
- People tagged friends and colleagues who were considering a career change

**Reusable Pattern:**
Start at the beginning not the outcome. Include a moment of doubt or difficulty. Let the person speak in their own words. End with a specific outcome not a general improvement.

### Post 006
**Client Type:** Industrial and Manufacturing
**ICP Target:** Industrial Business Communications Lead
**Platform:** LinkedIn
**Content Type:** Capability and certification announcement
**Performance:** 3,200 impressions, 89 reactions, 12 comments, 6 direct procurement enquiries
**Published:** 2025-03-05

**Content Summary:**
A post announcing ISO 9001 recertification with a focus on what the process revealed about internal quality systems rather than the certificate itself. Included a specific operational improvement identified during the audit.

**Why It Worked:**
- Did not lead with the certificate, led with what it meant operationally
- The specific improvement detail showed genuine process rigour
- Procurement managers commented that this level of transparency builds supplier confidence
- Tone was measured and precise without being dry
- Avoided all marketing language, read like an operational update not a press release

**Reusable Pattern:**
For industrial clients announcing certifications or milestones, lead with operational meaning not the award. Include one specific detail that demonstrates process depth. Keep tone precise and understated.

**ICP Target:** Community Organisation Manager
**Platform:** Facebook

**Performance:** 1,870 reach, 298 reactions, 67 comments, 19 new membership enquiries
**Published:** 2025-01-08

### Post 008
**Client Type:** Health and Wellness
**ICP Target:** Health and Wellness Professional
**Platform:** Instagram
**Content Type:** Myth busting educational post
**Performance:** 2,340 likes, 187 saves, 94 comments, 41 new booking enquiries
**Published:** 2025-02-28

**Content Summary:**
A post from a physiotherapist debunking the common belief that rest is always the best treatment for back pain. Led with the myth stated plainly. Followed with a calm evidence based explanation. Closed with a practical alternative and a soft call to book a consultation.

**Why It Worked:**
- The myth was stated as most people actually believe it, not as a strawman
- The correction was calm and authoritative without being condescending
- No medical claims, framed as professional perspective not diagnosis
- Saves were high indicating people wanted to reference it again
- Generated questions in comments that the practitioner answered, building further trust

**Reusable Pattern:**
State the myth exactly as people believe it. Correct it calmly with evidence or professional experience. Offer a practical takeaway. Close softly without a hard sell.
