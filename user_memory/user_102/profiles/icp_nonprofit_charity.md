# ICP Profile: Nonprofit and Charity Communications Lead

## Role Summary
Communications or fundraising lead at a small to mid size nonprofit, charity, or social enterprise. Could be in healthcare support, mental health, environment, homelessness, youth work, food poverty, or any cause driven organisation. Often a small team or solo role. Responsible for donor communications, volunteer recruitment, awareness campaigns, and maintaining public trust. Deeply mission driven but under-resourced.

## Core Pain Points
- Must produce emotionally resonant content without exploiting beneficiaries
- Very limited budget means no agency support
- Needs to speak to multiple audiences simultaneously, donors, volunteers, beneficiaries, and policymakers
- Burnout is real, wearing too many hats
- Maintaining consistent communications around seasonal fundraising peaks is overwhelming

## Content That Resonates
- Empathetic, human and dignity preserving tone
- Impact led storytelling with real numbers where possible
- Volunteer and donor spotlights
- Transparent behind the scenes of the work
- Urgent but not manipulative calls to action
- Accessible language that works for broad public audiences

## Buying Triggers
- Major fundraising campaign approaching
- New service or programme launching that needs awareness
- Annual report season requiring digestible public content
- Struggling to retain donors between campaigns
- Trustee or board asking for improved digital presence

## Content Channels
- Email newsletters to donor base
- Facebook primary for older donor demographic
- Instagram for volunteer recruitment and younger audiences
- LinkedIn for corporate partnerships and grant visibility
- Website blog for SEO and credibility
